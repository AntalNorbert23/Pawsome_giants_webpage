About us

Our cattery is based near the border with Hungary, in the north west of Romania, between Satu Mare and Oradea. This is the place we call home and we share with our coonies."We" includes me, my husband and my girls. Our babies grow up completely integrated in a household with dogs too and therefore will be used to lots of company. Our whole house is their playground and they can roam around in our spacious house.

Starting at the age of 14 to 16 weeks, after they got their shots and have been dewormed and declared healthy, they are ready to move to responsible adoptive parents.

My huge love for animals begin in my childhood, living alone with my parents, so having no siblings made me look after playmates. Since I'm quite an introverted person, the animals were very suitable for me, especially cats and dogs.

The Maine Coon breed caught my husband's attention at a cat show, where we accidentally went, because it was held in a huge shopping mall. So we decided with the girls to get a Maine Coon cat for my husband's 50th birthday. After that, things just happened and at the moment, we can't imagine our life without them anymore.

We are WCF registered.

About the Breed

The ancestral origins of the Maine Coon are unknown, but are speculated to be descended from Norwegian Forest Cats or Siberian Forest Cats, that may have been brought to New England by settlers.

First recorded Maine Coon cat in literature was in 1861 with a mention of a black and white cat named "Captain Jenks of the Horse Marines". Maine Coons were popular competitors at early cat shows in Boston and New York. A brown tabby female named "Cosie" won Best Cat at the 1895 Madison Square Garden Show.

The Maine Coon is a massive cat with a powerful muscular athletic body in keeping with their impressive size. They have large, pointed ears held wide and tall and also an intelligent expression.

Maine Coons develop slowly, and dont't achieve their full size until they are three to five years old. The Maine Coon is a long or medium haired cat. The coat is soft and silky, althought texture may vary with coat color. The length is shorter on the head and shoulders but longer on the stomach and flanks, with some cats having a leonine ruff around their neck.

Maine Coon can have any colors that other cats have. Colors indicating crossbreeding, such as chocolate, lavender, the Siamese pointed patterns or the "ticked" patterns, are not accepted by some breed standards. The most common pattern seen in the breed is brown tabby.

The Maine Coon cat is well known for its loving nature, kind disposition and great intelligence. These cats are especially good with children, dogs and other cats.

The Maine Coon is:

⏵ Sociable and dependent cat

⏵ Highly active and inquisitive cat

⏵ Slightly talkative cat

⏵ Great family cat

⏵ Great for first-time cat owners

⏵ Can cope with a busy household

⏵ Enjoys playing games and is active at home
